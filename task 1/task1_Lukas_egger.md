## 1. Topic & Domain
**Domain:** Finance / Quantitative Research (Algorithmic Trading)  
**Topic:** Investigating Demographic Bias in AI-driven Sentiment Analysis of Corporate Earnings Calls.  

## 2. Theoretical Framework (Course Connection)
This project applies the concepts of **I-Methodology** and **Intersectionality** to algorithmic finance. *I-Methodology* helps analyze whether LLMs implicitly assume a "default" corporate persona (typically male and Western) based on their training data, potentially penalizing deviations from this norm. *Intersectionality* will be operationalized by testing overlapping axes of identity (e.g., varying both gender and ethnicity simultaneously) to measure if combined demographic markers amplify biased risk-scoring in financial contexts.

## 3. Research Question
*"In automated sentiment analysis of corporate reports (earnings calls), do large language models systematically interpret identical financial statements as more negative or riskier when the executive’s demographic characteristics (gender, ethnicity) are varied?"*

## 4. Literature & Sources

* **Kim, A., Muhn, M., & Nikolaev, V. (2024).** *Financial Statement Analysis with Large Language Models.* *Link:* https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.17866
  *Relevance:* This paper demonstrates that LLMs possess human-level capabilities in analyzing complex financial statements without domain-specific training. It provides the foundational proof that using LLMs for financial evaluation is a highly relevant, real-world industry use case.

* **Zhong, H., Chen, S., & Liang, M. (2024).** *Gender Bias of LLM in Economics: An Existentialism Perspective.* *Link:* https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.19775
  *Relevance:* The authors provide robust empirical evidence that LLMs reproduce gender stereotypes in economic decision-making scenarios. This mathematically supports the core hypothesis that financial language evaluation by AI is inherently susceptible to demographic bias.

* **Lopez-Lira, A., & Tang, Y. (2023).** *Can ChatGPT Forecast Stock Price Movements? Return Predictability and Large Language Models.* *Link:* https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4412788
  *Relevance:* This study establishes how LLMs are actively utilized to forecast stock movements based on text sentiment. It bridges the gap between language processing and quantitative trading, illustrating the severe market impact if the underlying AI sentiment scores are biased.
